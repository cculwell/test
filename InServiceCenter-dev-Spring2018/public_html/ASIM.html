Alabama Science in Motion

In May of 1994, the Alabama State Legislature appropriated 1.97 million dollars to establish the Alabama Science in Motion (ASIM) program to provide significant laboratory experiences for all students enrolled in high school biology, chemistry, and physics classes. There are now eleven ASIM sites in Alabama and each is housed at one of the eleven regional in-service centers throughout the state. The Athens State ASIM site has three specialists and three vans (one each for biology, chemistry, and physics) and each van is equipped with over $90,000 worth of state-of-the-art equipment. The vans are driven by the specialists who are certified science teachers with extensive classroom experience. ASIM-Athens serves every high school in Region 2 which includes the following systems: Athens City, Blount County, Cullman City, Cullman County, Lawrence County, Limestone County, and Oneonta City.
